Idli Ingredients

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Steps to Make Idli

  1. Wash idli rice and urad dal separately. Soak them in water for 6-8 hours or overnight, along with fenugreek seeds.
  2. Drain the water from soaked rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds. Transfer them to a blender and grind into a smooth batter by adding water as needed.
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl. Add salt and mix well.
  4. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or until it doubles in volume.
  5. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee. Pour the fermented batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full.
  6. Steam the idlis in a steamer for 10-12 minutes or until they are cooked through and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Remove the idli molds from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes.
  8. Using a spoon or a knife, gently remove the idlis from the molds.
  9. Serve hot idlis with coconut chutney and sambar.

Dosa Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1/4 cup poha (flattened rice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps to Make Dosa

  1. Wash rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds together. Soak them in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the water and transfer the soaked ingredients to a blender. Add poha and blend into a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl. Add salt and mix well. Let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume.
  4. Heat a non-stick or cast iron griddle (tawa) over medium-high heat. Once hot, sprinkle a few drops of water on the surface. If it sizzles and evaporates, the tawa is ready.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer.
  6. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the bottom side is golden brown and crisp.
  7. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook for another minute on the other side.
  8. Remove the dosa from the tawa and serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any desired accompaniment.

Ven Pongal Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup split yellow moong dal
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 cashew nuts
  • Salt to taste

Steps to Make Ven Pongal

  1. Wash rice and moong dal together, then soak them in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker, heat ghee. Add cumin seeds and black peppercorns. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped ginger, green chilies, and asafoetida. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add cashew nuts and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add the drained rice and dal mixture. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add water and salt. Stir well.
  7. Close the pressure cooker lid and cook on medium heat for 4-5 whistles, or until the pongal is soft and mushy.
  8. Once the pressure releases, open the lid and mix the pongal well. Adjust consistency by adding more hot water if needed.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Medu Vadai Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps to Make Medu Vadai

  1. Wash urad dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 4-5 hours.
  2. Drain the water from soaked urad dal and transfer it to a blender or a food processor.
  3. Add green chilies, ginger, coriander leaves, peppercorns, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and salt to the blender.
  4. Blend the mixture into a coarse paste, adding very little water if necessary. The batter should be thick and fluffy.
  5. Transfer the batter to a bowl and mix it well with your hands to incorporate air.
  6. Heat oil for deep frying in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat.
  7. To shape the vadai, wet your hands with water. Take a small portion of the batter and shape it into a ball. Flatten it slightly to form a disc. Make a hole in the center using your thumb.
  8. Gently slide the shaped vadai into the hot oil. Fry 2-3 vadai at a time, depending on the size of your pan.
  9. Fry the vadai on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  10. Remove the vadai from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  12. Serve hot and crispy Medu Vadai with coconut chutney or sambar.

Upma Ingredients

  1. 1 cup semolina (sooji or rava)
  2. 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  3. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  4. 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  5. 1 teaspoon chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  7. 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  8. 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  9. 1 onion, finely chopped
  10. 1 carrot, finely chopped
  11. 1/4 cup green peas
  12. 2 cups water
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Steps to Make Upma

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add urad dal, chana dal, and cumin seeds. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  3. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  5. Add chopped carrot and green peas. Sauté for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add semolina (sooji) and roast it on medium-low heat until it turns aromatic and lightly golden brown. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
  7. Meanwhile, boil water in a separate pan.
  8. Slowly add the boiling water to the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  9. Add salt and mix well.
  10. Cover and cook on low heat for 5-7 minutes or until the upma is cooked and water is absorbed.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  12. Serve hot as is or with chutney.

Uttapam Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 capsicum, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • Handful of coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps to Make Uttapam

  1. Wash rice and urad dal separately, then soak them in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked rice and urad dal to a smooth batter consistency. Add semolina and yogurt to the batter. Mix well and let it ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  3. After fermentation, add salt to the batter and mix well.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle and lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it into a thick circle.
  6. Sprinkle chopped onion, tomato, capsicum, green chilies, and coriander leaves evenly over the top.
  7. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the uttapam. Cook on medium heat until the bottom turns golden brown.
  8. Flip the uttapam and cook the other side until it is cooked through.
  9. Remove from the pan and serve hot with coconut chutney or tomato chutney.

Aappam Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw rice
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Steps to Make Aappam

  1. Wash the raw rice and soak it in water for 4-5 hours.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the active dry yeast with lukewarm water and sugar. Let it sit for 10 minutes until frothy.
  3. Grind the soaked raw rice along with cooked rice, grated coconut, and yeast mixture to a smooth batter.
  4. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  5. Heat an appam pan over medium heat and grease it lightly with oil.
  6. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan and swirl it around quickly to spread the batter thinly towards the edges.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and cook the appam for 2-3 minutes until the edges turn golden brown and crispy, and the center is cooked through.
  8. Remove the appam from the pan and repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  9. Serve hot with coconut milk, stew, or any desired accompaniment.

Idiyappam Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 1/2 cups hot water
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Steps to Make Idiyappam

  1. In a mixing bowl, add rice flour and salt. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add hot water to the rice flour mixture while mixing with a spoon.
  3. Knead the mixture into a soft, pliable dough. Add more water if necessary.
  4. Grease the idiyappam maker (also called idiyappam press) with coconut oil.
  5. Fill the idiyappam maker with the dough.
  6. Press the dough onto idli plates or muslin cloth in a circular motion to form the idiyappam strands.
  7. Steam the idiyappam for about 8-10 minutes until cooked.
  8. Once cooked, carefully remove the idiyappam from the plates or cloth.
  9. Serve hot with coconut milk, stew, or any curry of your choice.

Full Meal Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup split white urad dal (optional)
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely grated
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup grated cucumber
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 cup cooked and shredded chicken breast or paneer cubes for vegetarian option
  • 1/2 cup cooked green peas
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mixed vegetables (like bell peppers, carrots, beans)
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts or cashews (optional for garnish)

Steps to Make Full Meal

  1. Wash rice and split white urad dal together, then soak them in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Cook the rice and dal in a rice cooker or on the stove with 3 cups of water until they are soft and mushy. Let it cool.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped green chilies, grated ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Add grated carrot, grated cucumber, and mixed vegetables. Sauté for another minute.
  7. If using chicken or paneer, add it to the pan and cook until it's slightly browned.
  8. Add a pinch of asafoetida and mix well.
  9. Add the cooked rice and dal mixture to the pan. Mix well with the tempering.
  10. Add salt to taste and mix again.
  11. Let the mixture cool slightly. Then, add plain yogurt (curd) and mix well.
  12. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and roasted peanuts or cashews, if desired.
  13. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  14. Serve chilled as a wholesome and refreshing full meal.

Sambar Rice Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (beans, peas, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Steps to Make Sambar Rice

  1. Wash rice and toor dal together, then soak them in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker, heat oil. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Add chopped onions and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  6. Add chopped vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add sambar powder and salt. Mix well.
  8. Add the drained rice and dal mixture. Stir well to coat with the spices.
  9. Add water and stir again.
  10. Close the pressure cooker lid and cook on medium heat for 4-5 whistles, or until rice and dal are cooked.
  11. Once the pressure releases, open the lid and mix the Sambar Rice well. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with papad or raita.

Vegetable Biryani Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes, cauliflower, etc.), diced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

Steps to Make Vegetable Biryani

  1. Wash the basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  6. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for 5-7 minutes.
  7. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, biryani masala, salt, chopped mint leaves, and chopped coriander leaves. Mix well.
  8. Add yogurt and mix well. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  9. Add soaked basmati rice and water. Stir gently.
  10. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and the water is absorbed, about 15-20 minutes.
  11. Once done, let it sit covered for another 5 minutes.
  12. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork and serve hot with raita or salad.

Parotta Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Water, as needed
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter), for cooking

    • Steps to Make Parotta

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and salt.
    • Add vegetable oil and mix well.
    • Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a smooth, soft dough.
    • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and let them rest for about 15-20 minutes, covered with a damp cloth.
    • Take one dough ball and roll it out into a thin, elongated shape using a rolling pin.
    • Brush the rolled-out dough with ghee.
    • Starting from one end, roll the dough into a tight spiral.
    • Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
    • Take each spiral-shaped dough and flatten it gently with your palm.
    • Heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat.
    • Place a flattened dough on the hot griddle and cook until both sides are golden brown, brushing with ghee as needed.
    • Repeat the cooking process with the remaining flattened dough.
    • Serve hot with your favorite curry or side dish.

Butter Naan Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Steps to Make Butter Naan

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda.
  2. Add plain yogurt and vegetable oil. Mix well.
  3. Gradually add warm milk and knead the mixture into a smooth, soft dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 2 hours in a warm place.
  5. After the resting period, divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  6. Preheat the oven to the highest setting (usually around 500°F or 260°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside.
  7. Roll out each dough ball into an oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  8. Brush one side of the rolled-out dough with melted butter.
  9. Place the buttered side of the dough on the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven.
  10. Bake for 2-3 minutes, or until the naan puffs up and the bottom is golden brown.
  11. Remove from the oven and brush the top with more melted butter.
  12. Serve hot with your favorite curry or side dish.

Kottu Parotta Ingredients

  • 4 parottas (pre-made or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, peas, etc.), finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 eggs (optional)

Steps to Make Kottu Parotta

  1. Shred the parottas into small pieces using your hands or a knife. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and sauté until they are slightly tender.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  7. Stir in turmeric powder, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  8. Add the shredded parottas to the pan. Mix everything together until the parottas are well coated with the masala.
  9. If using eggs, push the parotta mixture to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until cooked.
  10. Combine the scrambled eggs with the parotta mixture.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot as is or with a side of raita or curry.

Paneer Butter Masala Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps to Make Paneer Butter Masala

  1. Heat butter and oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Meanwhile, grind the soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste.
  6. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala to the pan. Cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add the cashew paste and mix well.
  8. Once the mixture thickens, add cream and mix well.
  9. Add cubed paneer and salt to taste. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the gravy.
  10. Cover and cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to blend.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with naan, rice, or any Indian bread of your choice.

Chapathi Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps to Make Chapathi

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it is smooth and pliable. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls.
  4. Take one dough ball and roll it into a circle using a rolling pin. Dust with flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  5. Heat a tawa (flat griddle) over medium-high heat.
  6. Place the rolled chapati on the hot tawa and cook until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  7. Flip the chapathi and cook the other side until brown spots start to appear.
  8. Brush the cooked chapati with oil or ghee and press gently with a spatula to puff it up.
  9. Remove the chapathi from the tawa and repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  10. Serve hot with your favorite curry or side dish.

Chicken Manchurian Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, for frying
  • For the sauce:
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, dissolved in 1/4 cup water
  • 1 cup chicken broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 spring onions, chopped, for garnish

Steps to Make Chicken Manchurian

  1. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornstarch, egg, garlic paste, ginger paste, soy sauce, chili sauce, black pepper powder, and salt to make a marinade.
  2. Add chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a pan or wok for frying.
  4. Fry the marinated chicken pieces in batches until they are golden brown and cooked through. Drain them on paper towels and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil.
  6. Add chopped onions, diced green bell pepper, slit green chilies, minced garlic, and chopped ginger. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  7. Add soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Stir well.
  8. Add chicken broth or water and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  9. Gradually add the cornstarch-water mixture while stirring continuously until the sauce thickens.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Add the fried chicken pieces to the sauce and toss until they are well coated.
  12. Garnish with chopped spring onions.
  13. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.

Chicken Chettinad Ingredients

  • 500g bone-in chicken pieces
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Steps to Make Chicken Chettinad

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add fennel seeds, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and black peppercorns. Sauté until aromatic.
  2. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add chicken pieces and coat them well with the masala.
  7. Cover and cook the chicken until it's tender and cooked through, stirring occasionally.
  8. Once the chicken is cooked, add coconut milk and simmer for a few minutes.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with rice or roti.
